Carmelite Saints

A brief introduction to some of the principal saints who model the Carmelite way of life.

Mary, Our Lady of Mount Carmel

Model of contemplation and faithful discipleship. Her mantle shelters all who seek her Son.

St. Teresa of Jesus

Doctor of the Church; teacher of prayer and reformer of Carmel.

St. John of the Cross

Mystical doctor guiding souls through the night to union with God.

St. Thérèse of the Child Jesus (St. Thérèse of Lisieux)

Doctor of the Church who taught the “Little Way” of spiritual childhood—confidence and love in the ordinary.

St. Elizabeth of the Trinity

Mystic of the indwelling Trinity who lived as a “praise of glory,” inviting us to recollection and interior silence.

St. Teresa Benedicta of the Cross (Edith Stein)

Philosopher, convert, Carmelite nun, and martyr who witnessed to truth and the Cross in the face of persecution.

St. Teresa of Jesus of the Andes

Young Chilean Carmelite who radiated joy and simplicity, offering ordinary life entirely to Christ.

St. Titus Brandsma

Carmelite priest, journalist, and martyr; defender of the dignity of the human person and freedom of the press.

St. Raphael Kalinowski

Polish Carmelite priest who rebuilt the Order after suppression; model of perseverance, mercy, and reconciliation.

St. Mary of Jesus Crucified (Mariam Baouardy)

Humble Carmelite from the Holy Land known for childlike trust, mystical graces, and love of the Holy Spirit.

St. Teresa Margaret of the Sacred Heart

Hidden saint of charity whose silent life in community was consumed by love for the Heart of Jesus.

St. Simon Stock

Early Carmelite associated with the tradition of the Brown Scapular, a sign of Marian devotion and commitment.

St. Albert of Jerusalem

Lawgiver of Carmel who gave the Rule, shaping the Order’s life of prayer, fraternity, and service in allegiance to Christ.
