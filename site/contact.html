Meeting Information

What to Expect at Your First Meeting

  • Arrival: Please arrive by 9:30 AM. We begin promptly with communal prayer.
  • Opening Prayer: We pray Morning Prayer from the Liturgy of the Hours together.
  • Formation Talk: A presentation on Carmelite spirituality, prayer, or the life of a Carmelite saint.
  • Lectio Divina: Group practice of sacred reading and reflection on Scripture.
  • Community Sharing: Time for fraternal conversation, questions, and announcements.
  • Closing Prayer: We conclude with prayer around 11:00 AM.

First-time visitors are always welcome! No preparation or prior knowledge is necessary. Simply come with an open heart.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be Catholic to attend?

Yes, membership in the Secular Order of Discalced Carmelites requires being a practicing Catholic in good standing with the Church. However, those who are exploring the Catholic faith or in RCIA are welcome to attend meetings as guests.

Is there a cost to join?

There are no membership dues, but members are encouraged to support the community through voluntary contributions to cover costs of materials, meeting space, and occasional events. Financial concerns should never prevent anyone from joining.

How long does formation take?

The formation process typically takes 5-6 years from inquiry to Definitive Promise. This includes periods of inquiry, aspirancy, initial formation, and ongoing formation. Each person progresses at their own pace.

Can I attend if I'm married with a family?

Absolutely! The OCDS is specifically for lay people living in the world. Many of our members are married, have children, work full-time, or have other commitments. The Carmelite vocation is about bringing contemplative prayer into everyday life.

What if I can't attend every monthly meeting?

We understand that life circumstances sometimes prevent attendance. While regular participation is important for formation and community life, we work with each member's situation. Speak with the formation director about your particular needs.

Do I need to have experience with contemplative prayer?

Not at all! Many people come to us precisely because they want to learn about contemplative prayer and the interior life. Formation provides guidance and teaching on mental prayer, Lectio Divina, and growing in union with God.
